Why not just buy a timer?

A timer waters on a schedule whether the soil needs it or not — it floods beds after rain and still leaves them dry in a heatwave. This system checks moisture in each bed independently and waters only what's thirsty, in the cool of the morning and evening, off one small solar panel with no mains connection at all.

Questions

Do I need electronics or coding experience?

No. Everything runs at 12V (like a car — nothing here can shock you), and the software is provided complete; you copy it onto the Pi and change a few labelled settings.

Can I run it from a tap instead of a water tank?

Yes — drop the pump, keep the valves, and fit a pressure reducer. The guide covers both versions.

I only have two beds, or eight — does it still work?

Yes, the design and the spreadsheet's calculators scale either way. It also works beautifully on a single balcony planter with one zone.
