# Pricing & Launch Offer

Mirrors `product-source/LaunchKit.md` — that's the source of truth, this is
here so pricing shows up alongside the rest of this project's structure.

## Standing price

**£12.99**, single listing (build guide PDF + parts list spreadsheet with
sizing calculators, both files in one purchase — no tiers). Comparable DIY
build plans on Etsy run roughly £6-12, with premium comprehensive plans at
£25-30; this one includes working software and live calculators, which the
woodworking-style plans in that range don't, so £12.99 is mid-premium and
defensible on that basis.

## Launch offer (first month)

**£9.99**, clearly marked as launch pricing rising to £12.99 after the
first month.

## What you actually keep per sale

- **Etsy**: after listing fee, 6.5% transaction fee, and payment
  processing, expect to keep roughly £11 of a £12.99 sale (check current
  fee rates when you sign up — they change).
- **Gumroad**: flat ~10% fee, so roughly £11.70 of £12.99.
- Both platforms handle VAT on consumer digital sales for you.

## Why one listing, not tiers

The LaunchKit's plan is one file bundle at one price, not a Core/Plus/
Bundle ladder — email support for buyers is already implicit ("message me,
I built this thing and I answer"), so there's no separate paid support
tier to manage. Keep it this way until there's a clear reason to split it
(e.g. a second, more advanced product — see Section 5 of the LaunchKit on
stacking uni notes on Stuvia as a top-up, which is a separate listing, not
a tier of this one).

## Money-in-hand math

- Month 1 is realistically **£0-40** while the first reviews build — the
  LaunchKit is explicit about this, and it matches how digital-download
  marketplaces actually work (search doesn't surface a review-less listing).
- Months 3-6 is where **£100-200/month** becomes realistic, driven by the
  DesignSpark article + video funnel plus accumulated reviews.
- The bar isn't breakeven — the guide already exists. The bar is whether
  reviews and traffic are visibly compounding by month 3; if they're flat,
  see the pivot metric in `../OPERATIONS.md`.
