1. Open the build guide docx and fill every yellow box: your photos, your wiring diagram, your fertigation mass balance tables, your code if it differs. Most of this already exists in your DesignSpark draft.

## 4. First 30 days: getting the first sales

Digital listings don't sell from search alone at first. Reviews compound, so the job in month one is seeding the first ten buyers.

1. **End the DesignSpark article with one line:** "Full build plans, parts list and code are available here" with the link. That article is your single best traffic source: exactly the right audience at exactly the right moment.
2. **One 60-second vertical video** of the system doing a watering round, posted to YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels and TikTok with the link in bio. The top sellers in this niche all pair plans with video.
3. **Gardening and allotment Facebook groups:** share it as a story ("our student community garden won an engineering award for this, here's how it works"), not an advert, and follow each group's promo rules.
4. **The award angle is genuinely newsworthy:** EWB Strathclyde socials, IChemE student channels, and the university comms team all have reasons to share it, and every share is free traffic.
5. Answer every buyer message fast and ask happy builders for a review with a photo. Ten photo reviews is what turns a listing from invisible to self-sustaining.

## 5. The boring but important bits

- **Tax:** the UK trading allowance makes your first £1,000 of gross sales per tax year tax-free with no paperwork. If this works to plan you'll pass that within the year, at which point you register for Self Assessment. Worth doing properly from the start: keep a one-tab spreadsheet of sales.
- **Support load:** expect the odd buyer question. Budget ten minutes a day at most; the troubleshooting section exists to absorb most of it.
- **Honest expectations:** month one is usually £0 to £40 while reviews build. Months three to six is where £100 to £200 becomes realistic if the video and article funnel traffic. Stacking your uni notes on Stuvia later is the easy top-up toward the £200 end.
