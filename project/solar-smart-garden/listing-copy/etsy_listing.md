## Description

BUILD A GARDEN THAT WATERS ITSELF

These are the complete plans for a solar-powered, self-watering vegetable
garden controlled by a Raspberry Pi, exactly as designed and built at the
award-winning Strathclyde Community Garden (Winner, IChemE Young Engineers
Award for Innovation and Sustainability).

A small solar panel keeps a battery charged. The Pi checks the soil in
each bed and waters only the beds that need it, in the cool of the morning
and evening. No plug, no timer to reset, no dead courgettes after a week
away.

**WHAT YOU GET (instant download)**
- 17-page step-by-step build guide with real photos from the working system
- Complete, working Python controller code, ready to copy onto your Pi
- Full parts list spreadsheet with typical UK prices and a tick-off column
- Solar panel and battery sizing calculators (just type in your own numbers)
- Watering calculator, commissioning checklist, seasonal care plan and a
  full troubleshooting guide
- Bonus sections: per-bed liquid feeding (fertigation) and free
  recycled-bottle micro-greenhouses

**THE DETAILS**
- Skill level: confident beginner. No electronics or coding experience assumed
- Everything runs at 12V (safe extra-low voltage, like a car)
- Build time: two weekends
- Parts cost: roughly £265 to £360 from any online electronics and garden suppliers
- Waters up to 5 beds independently; scales down to a single balcony planter
- Running cost: nothing. It's the sun

Instant digital download. Files are available the moment payment
