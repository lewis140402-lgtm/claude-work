# Organic posts for Facebook allotment/gardening groups, EWB Strathclyde, IChemE student channels

Per the LaunchKit's plan: lead with the award/story angle for engineering
and university channels (genuinely newsworthy, free to share), and a
practical "here's what we built" story — not an advert — for gardening
groups. Check each group's self-promotion rules first.

## Facebook allotment/gardening groups

**Post title/opener**
Our student community garden won an engineering award for this — here's
how it waters itself

**Post body**

A few of us built a solar-powered, Raspberry Pi-controlled watering system
for our community garden at Strathclyde — it checks the soil in each bed
and only waters what's actually dry, running entirely off one small solar
panel. It won the IChemE Young Engineers Award for Innovation and
Sustainability this year.

Wrote up the full build (parts list, wiring, the actual code) as a guide
in case anyone else wants to build their own version — happy to answer
sizing/setup questions in the comments either way.

## Notes
- Personalise the specific failure/lesson mentioned in any follow-up
  comments using the real troubleshooting section in the build guide
  (e.g. sensor calibration, relay active-high/low mixup, blown fuse) —
  don't invent an anecdote that didn't happen.
- Space posts across different groups/channels by a few days rather than
  posting everywhere on day one.
