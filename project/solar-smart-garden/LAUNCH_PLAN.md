# Launch Plan

This mirrors `product-source/LaunchKit.md` section 6's checklist, expanded
with how it connects to the rest of this project (the video pipeline,
`OPERATIONS.md`'s weekly rhythm). Where this and the LaunchKit disagree on
a specific instruction, follow the LaunchKit.

## Week 0 — finish the product (one evening, per LaunchKit Section 1)
- [ ] Fill every yellow box in `product-source/SolarSmartGardenBuildGuide.docx` (your photos, wiring diagram, fertigation mass balance tables, production code if it differs)
- [ ] Delete the "Read me first" page, update the award year if wanted, export to PDF
- [ ] Quick courtesy checks: agree with Reuben how you're handling this since the garden was a joint project; skim the RS Big Pitch / Alumni Fund terms for anything about commercial spin-offs
- [ ] Shoot the photos and 30-second video from the LaunchKit's shot list (hero shot, PDF cover, enclosure interior, dripper close-up, panel mounted, bottle greenhouse, watering log, 30s video of a watering round)

## Week 1 — go live
- [ ] Etsy: open the shop, publish the listing using the LaunchKit's title/tags/description/price (£9.99 launch), upload both files + all photos + the video
- [ ] Gumroad: mirror the same listing (ten minutes, per LaunchKit Section 3)
- [ ] End the DesignSpark article with the link — per the LaunchKit, this is the single best traffic source: exactly the right audience at exactly the right moment
- [ ] Post the 30-second video to YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and TikTok with the link in bio
- [ ] Share once in two gardening/allotment Facebook groups as a story ("our student community garden won an engineering award for this"), not an advert — follow each group's promo rules
- [ ] Share with EWB Strathclyde and IChemE student channels, and the university comms team — the award angle is genuinely newsworthy and every share is free traffic
- [ ] Create the one-tab sales tracking spreadsheet

## Week 2 — sustain it
- [ ] Answer every buyer message fast; ask happy builders for a review with a photo — ten photo reviews is what turns the listing from invisible to self-sustaining
- [ ] If the video pipeline (`../video-pipeline/`) is running by now, start publishing additional short videos beyond the one launch video — more surface area pointing at the same Etsy/Gumroad links, same niche audience
- [ ] Log every sale in the tracking spreadsheet regardless of the £1,000 trading allowance (see `../OPERATIONS.md`) — you want clean records from day one

## Weeks 3-8 — steady rhythm
- [ ] Move to the operating loop in `../OPERATIONS.md`: daily 10-15 minute session, weekly numbers review
- [ ] Budget ~10 minutes/day for buyer support (LaunchKit Section 5) — the troubleshooting section in the guide exists to absorb most of it
- [ ] Track reviews and traffic against the honest month-by-month expectations in `PRICING.md`: £0-40 in month 1, £100-200/month becoming realistic by months 3-6

## What "launch worked" looks like
Per the LaunchKit's own honest expectations: don't expect much in month 1
— that's normal for a review-less listing. The real signal is whether
reviews and traffic are visibly compounding by month 3. If they're flat by
then, that's the pivot-check point in `../OPERATIONS.md`, not before.
