# Operations Manual

## The daily session (5-10 hrs/week total — roughly 45-70 min/day)

### What you paste to me
Pick ONE of these each session, in order of priority if you're short on time:

1. **Video day**: `"Generate today's video: <topic from NICHE_AND_TOPICS.md, or a new one>"`
   — I write the script.json, you (or I, if you give me shell access that
   session) run `make_video.py`, you review the output and post it.
2. **Guide/product day**: `"Here's feedback/questions from buyers this week: <paste>"`
   — I turn it into V2 content fixes, FAQ additions, or new social posts.
3. **Social day**: `"Post for today"` — I pull the next item from
   `solar-irrigation-guide/social-posts/30_DAYS_POSTS.md`, personalise it
   with anything you tell me happened this week, and hand you the exact text.
4. **Review day** (weekly, not daily): `"Weekly numbers: sales <n>, revenue
   £<n>, video views <n>"` — I update the milestone tracker below and tell
   you straight whether you're on pace.

### What I produce, every time
- Finished text/copy/code — never a half-draft you have to finish.
- One clear next action if something needs your judgement (a reply only you
  can send, a photo only you can take, a real number I don't have).
- A flag if something in the plan isn't working, instead of quietly
  continuing a dead approach.

### What only you can do (can't be delegated)
- Take real photos/footage of your actual build for video/social content
- Reply to buyer emails/DMs personally (Plus tier support is part of the
  product's value — don't let me ghostwrite it wholesale)
- Make the actual purchase/pricing decisions when they involve real money
- Post the content (I can draft; publishing is a deliberate human action)

## Week-by-week milestones (8 weeks)

| Week | Focus | Target |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Launch prep + network sales | Guide live, 3-5 warm-network sales |
| 2 | Organic push + first video | 10+ total sales, first video published |
| 3 | Steady rhythm | Daily video or post live, 15+ total sales |
| 4 | First data checkpoint | 20+ total sales OR clear signal on what's not working (see pivot metric) |
| 5 | Double down or adjust | Scale whichever channel (network/community/video) drove most sales |
| 6 | Content compounding | Video channel has 15-20 videos live, look for any breakout topic |
| 7 | Second product signal | Ask: does Plus-tier support volume justify a Bundle video tier? |
| 8 | Month-2 review | Compare actual vs Phase 1 month-2 estimate (£50-150); decide month 3-6 plan |

## The metric that decides double-down vs pivot

**By end of week 4: cost of one sale, in your time.**

Add up hours spent (content + support + admin) and divide by total sales.
- Under ~30 min/sale → double down, this channel scales with more volume
- 30 min-1.5 hrs/sale → keep going, but tighten the funnel (better landing
  page copy, sharper video hooks) before adding more volume
- Over 1.5 hrs/sale with no improving trend → the product or channel isn't
  working at your current distribution reach; revisit Phase 1's runner-up
  models (freelancing/tutoring convert faster short-term) rather than
  grinding a channel that isn't responding

Track sales count and £ revenue weekly — that's the only number that
matters early on. Video views/likes are a leading indicator, not a result;
don't let vanity metrics substitute for the sales count in this decision.

## UK tax — read this before your first sale

- **Trading Allowance**: the first **£1,000 of gross trading/casual income
  per tax year** (6 April-5 April) is tax-free and doesn't need to be
  reported to HMRC at all. This covers guide sales, video ad revenue, and
  any freelance income *combined* — it's one £1,000 allowance across all of
  it, not £1,000 per activity.
- **Once your gross income from this crosses £1,000 in a tax year**, you
  must register as self-employed with HMRC (by 5 October following the end
  of that tax year) and file a Self Assessment return declaring all of it
  — not just the amount over £1,000.
- Practically: track every sale from day one regardless of the allowance,
  so you're not reconstructing records under time pressure later. Register
  the moment you're confident you'll cross £1,000 in the tax year, don't
  wait until you actually cross it.
- This is general information, not tax advice — confirm your specific
  situation (especially once income is meaningful) with HMRC's guidance
  directly or an accountant.
- The **paper trading bot** (optional side project below) has no tax
  implications at all — it never touches real money, so there's no real
  income or gain to report, ever, unless you deliberately choose to go live
  later (out of scope here).
